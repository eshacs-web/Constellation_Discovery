About Constellations

It was during ancient times that constellations were first discovered, and early astronomers and cultures employed the night sky for navigating, keeping time, and developing mythologies. Early cultures such as the Sumerians, Egyptians, Greeks, and Chinese saw patterns of stars and organized them into constellations, most of which were often linked to legends or gods. It was the Greeks, spearheaded by those like Hipparchus and Ptolemy, who formalized many of the constellations that exist today. During centuries, technological improvements, including telescopes, made mapping the sky more accurately, which resulted in the discovery of new constellations, particularly in the Southern Hemisphere. Currently, 88 constellations are officially recognized by the International Astronomical Union, each defining a unique region of the night sky.
